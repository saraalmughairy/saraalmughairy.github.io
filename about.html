Product Background.

For as long as I can remember, I've been interested in human behavior. As a preteen, I used to binge-watch reality TV, analyzing the psychology behind the dramatics and making armchair diagnoses using my mom's DSM-IV. In college, I worked in neuropharmaceutical and cognitive science labs, which led to me running a cognitive development lab after graduation. But the more I learned about what makes people tick, the more I restless I became simply studying it. I wanted to take that knowledge and do something about it - I wanted to make things. And so instead of continuing into the field of academic research, I went back to school to study Human-Computer Interaction and Design, bridging my interests in people, business, and design to create user-centered products.

Design Philosophies.

Nothing exists in a vaccuum

My interdisciplinary background has led me to be a systems thinker, meaning I appreciate that there are direct and indirect use cases and stakeholders to consider when designing for even seemingly small interactions. Operating under this framework enables me to design strategically, with nuance and intention.

User-Driven, Data-Informed

I am a user-centered designer, meaning I believe that if you are empathetic to your users needs, your product will ultimately be superior. I also believe that it's important for characterizations of users to be grounded in user research.

ship often

Rapid iteration is key. As painful as it is to release something with flaws, iterating based on feedback from actual users is so powerful!

empower your community

Whether it's mentoring others or contributing to internal or external community projects, I've found that collaborating and empathizing with others strengthens my design approach.
